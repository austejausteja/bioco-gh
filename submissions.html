Parkinson's - a Prion-like disorder?

by Peggy, Medical Neuro BSc, Y2

A theory introduced by neuroanatomist Braak discusses the cause of PD originating from prions, protein aggregates caused by misfolding proteins at the brainstem and spreading to more neurons upwards to eventually affect the dopaminergic neurons (Braak et al., 2003. Figure 1). Besides prions, there are many other suggestive causes of PD, all linking to the dysfunction of dopamine and causing parkinsonian-like symptoms.

Human ATP-dependant DNA Helicase RecQ5

by Austeja, Biomedical Science MSci, Y2

Humans possess a RecQ family of DNA helicases. RecQ5 is an ATP-dependent 3’ to 5’ DNA helicase and shares some structural similarities with the other RecQ members through highly conserved protein domains (i.e. core helicase domain HD1+HD2). The unique protein motifs give the enzyme its distinctive functions that contribute to genome stability (Croteau et al., 2014).

Is Going BPA Free The Answer?

by Angela, Medical Neuro BSc, Y2

Bisphenol A is an oestrogen mimic found in polycarbonate plastics and epoxy resins. Threats of BPA to the mammalian reproductive system was first found out due to an accidental exposure of caged female mice in 1998. This exposure, caused their oocytes to exhibit high levels of defects in the chromosome alignment in the first meiotic spindle, termed as congressional failure (Patricia A. Hunt et al, 2003).
